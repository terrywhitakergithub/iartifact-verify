Who is this for? If someone sent you an iArtifact — a piece of music, visual art, writing, software, photography, film, a beat, a physical work, an NFT, a patent filing, a trademark, or any creative or professional record — this tool lets you independently confirm it is exactly what they say it is. You don't need an account. Nothing leaves your machine. Verification runs entirely in your browser.

What does it prove? That every file is byte-for-byte identical to what was recorded at creation, that the complete history linking every event is mathematically intact, and that the timestamp was signed by a trusted third party — not the creator. For AI-generated works, it also confirms EU Article 50 disclosure is present.

Outside the EU? The ⚑ EU AI Act disclosure is still meaningful worldwide — it satisfies international transparency standards for AI-generated content and is increasingly recognized by platforms, galleries, publishers, and regulators globally.

How verification works
01
File Integrity
Every file is fingerprinted in your browser and compared against the value locked in at creation time. Any alteration — even one byte — fails this check immediately.
02
Provenance Chain
Every event — create, scan, mint, stamp, seal — is recorded as a linked entry. Each one is mathematically bound to the previous. Tamper any entry and every entry after it breaks.
03
Identity Confirmation
The iA-ID is derived from the artifact's creation record. This verifier confirms it matches — proving the artifact has not been substituted or cloned from another record.
04
Trusted Timestamp
The timestamp token was issued and signed by a public authority — not the creator. It cannot be backdated. It proves the files existed at that exact moment in time, permanently.
05
⚑ EU AI Act · Article 50
For AI-generated works, this verifier confirms that machine-readable disclosure is embedded and unaltered. Required under EU Regulation 2024/1689 Article 50 for any AI-generated content made available to the public. Compliant by design.
06
Zero Upload
Nothing is sent to any server. Your files are processed entirely inside your browser. No account, no tracking, no upload. The source code is public and auditable on GitHub.
